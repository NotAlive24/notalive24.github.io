9. The Goal of Business Continuity

Business Continuity

Business continuity means keeping the critical parts of an organization running during a disruption, even if they operate at a reduced capacity.

Causes of Disruption

It may be needed during disturbances, attacks, infrastructure failures, or natural disasters.

Minor vs Major Incidents

Most incidents are small and easy to fix, but major incidents can stop business for too long and require business continuity measures.

When Business Continuity Is Needed

It is used when a normal incident response is not enough to handle the disruption.

Main Focus

It focuses on keeping critical products and services available until normal business operations return.

Scope of Business Continuity

It includes planning, preparation, response, and recovery, but it does not usually cover full restoration of all services.

Reduced Operations

Important business areas may continue working at a lower level until recovery is complete.

Organizational Commitment

Creating a business continuity plan needs strong support in terms of people, time, and money.

Management Support

Executive management or an executive sponsor must support business continuity planning for it to succeed.

Simple Meaning

Business continuity helps an organization keep its most important functions running during major disruptions.
