14. Theoretical Example Code of Ethics

1. Retinal Scanner Discrimination Case

  • The organization misused biometric data collected through retinal scanners.
  • Sensitive information (gender and pregnancy status) was accessed for hiring decisions.
  • This resulted in discrimination against female candidates.
  • Violates cybersecurity code of ethics: acting dishonestly, unjustly, and irresponsibly.
  • Security systems must only be used for their intended purpose

2️2. Network Administrator Misuse of Authority

  • A user violated acceptable use policy (not a criminal offense).
  • The network administrator monitored the user without official assignment.
  • Personal conflict suggests abuse of authority.
  • Violates ethical principles of professionalism, trust, and responsible use of privileges.
  • Administrator’s actions may create legal and organizational risks.

Key Ethical Lesson

  • IT professionals must act legally, honestly, and responsibly.
  • Privileged access must never be used for personal benefit or discrimination.
  • Ethical violations by security professionals can cause greater harm than policy violations.
