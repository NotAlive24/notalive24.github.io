6. Protecting Information

  • While a single data point (like a birthdate) might be anonymous on its own, combining data elements creates Personally Identifiable Information (PII) that can pinpoint an individual.
  • Cybersecurity experts are ethically and professionally bound to protect this data using the CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability).
  • Security isn't just theoretical; it’s active in your daily life every time you check your bank balance, visit a doctor, or send an email.
  • Success in this field requires a "security-first" way of thinking—constantly evaluating how information is handled, stored, and defended.
