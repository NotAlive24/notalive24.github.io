4. Security Awareness Training

Security awareness training (teaching employees how to recognize and prevent security risks) helps ensure everyone understands their responsibilities (tasks they are expected to perform) and accountability (being responsible for their actions) when protecting an organization’s systems and data.

It also helps identify carelessness (lack of attention to security practices) and complacency (feeling security risks are not important) that could create security risks.

Three Learning Activities

Organizations use three main ways to teach security concepts:

Education

Education (learning general concepts and understanding security principles) helps people understand security ideas and apply them in real situations.

Training

Training (learning specific skills or procedures) focuses on teaching people how to perform security tasks, such as:

  • recognizing phishing emails
  • using secure passwords
  • following security procedures

Awareness

Awareness (making people aware of security risks and why they matter) focuses on gaining attention and encouraging people to think about security threats and safe behavior.

Key Idea

Before teaching detailed rules or skills, organizations often need to raise awareness first (make people understand that security issues exist) so that employees pay attention to security practices.

Main Idea:
Security awareness training ensures employees understand security risks, responsibilities, and correct behavior to help protect the organization.
